My Tennis Career

  How it all started

  I started playing tennis when covid ended. I needed to get out of the house and this was a perfect oppurtunity. When I first started playing, I was bad, really bad. I   originally started playing for fun, but once it escelated, I became more serious about it. I increased my playing from about 4 hours a week to almost 15 hours a week.   And that's where it all began.

  Modern Day Playing

  Ever since I started playing more, I got good. Not amazing, but good. My modern day schedule goes like this:

  • Playing every day for about 2-2.5 hours
  • Playing tournaments around once a month
  • Playing high school tennis matches starting in March

  My tennis playing schdule is what has made me become the player I have become today. Without all of this, I probably would not be as good as I am today.

  Where I Play/Who I Play With

  My tennis career started at The Sports Mall, an amazing tennis facility in Murray, UT. I originally started playing with my caoches and a few friends, but of course, I wasn't playing consecutively. I play at Sports Mall every singles day and, like I said, I play for about 2-2.5 hours each day. On the weekends, I either take a private lesson from my coach, play a tournament, or just hit with my dad or my friend named Joshya. Typically, during our hitting sessions we work on groundstrokes, volleys, and serves.

  My Strengths

When I play tennis, I have a lot of strengths, but with those strengths, come weaknesses. The things I am mostly good at are the following:

  • Forhands
  • Serves
  • Backhands
